Why I did it It paid for college I had to do something with my degree Nothing compares to how awesome it feels to help a bunch of kids learn
Storytime I broke the spacebar on my computer from using AutoCAD. Twice. When you have a class full of rowdy, interrupting, teenagers, sometimes you have to take them to the park and destroy them at wiffle ball. People say that an adult who enjoys beating a bunch of children in wiffle ball is selfish and immature. I can live with that.
Biggest takeaway Give me 5 special entrees of the day with their respective ingredients. Hear me fire them back at you like a food machine gun Making an abstract idea into something tangible is really hard People are wired to learn by first encountering a problem, then developing strategies to address it. In schools it's often backwards. We present information and strategies up front, and the 'problem' is a test days later.
